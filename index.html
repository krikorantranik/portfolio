Philosophy

Before getting into the technical aspects, these are some good philosophies that have helped me succeed so far as a data expert.

  • Know your goal and keep it always in sight: with data you want to tell a story, what is the story you want to tell? A clear research question will give you back a direction.
  • Be honest, direct, but sensible. Be conscious of the impact the results of your investigation might have on your audience, but tell the truth, no matter how hard. In this sense, the quality of the data or research may be questioned. Be confident in your work, but keep an open mind.
  • KISS: "Keep it simple stu...". Don't overcomplicate your delivery. The leaders want to see conclusions, the rest is your work as an analyst, not theirs. Let your results do the talking for you.
  • Remember Occam's Razor; of which I like Einstein's version: "The supreme goal of all theory is to make irreducible basic elements as simple and as few as possible without having to surrender the adequate representation of a single datum of experience".
  • Know and list all the assumptions you are making along the whole pipeline of data analytics. Refrain from using an algorithm you don't understand.
  • Know your biases too. We all have them. Keep your personal opinions outside of your research. If you at one point are having issues with this point, look for validation to confirm that your methods and conclusions are valid and that you are delivering them as neutrally and free of personal remarks as possible.
